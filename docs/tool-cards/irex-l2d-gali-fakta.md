# IREX Learn to Discern (L2D) / Gali Fakta

**Operator:** [irex.org](https://irex.org) | **Type:** Non-detector | **Cost:** Free

!!! abstract "TL;DR"
    IREX's flagship media-literacy methodology, adapted to over
    twenty country contexts. In Indonesia the deployment is
    Literata (the curriculum) plus Gali Fakta (the WhatsApp-
    group-chat-style game). The methodology backbone for any
    media-literacy programme a fact-checker deploys to audiences
, taught alongside the inoculation-game tradition rather
 than as a competitor to it.

## What it does

Learn to Discern is a media-literacy methodology developed by
IREX over more than a decade of cross-country deployment. The
curriculum teaches audiences how manipulative information works:
sourcing checks, emotional-language recognition, false-balance
patterns, image-and-video manipulation cues, through
participatory training, online campaigns, and game-based
practice. The methodology has been adapted to operational
environments including Ukraine, Serbia, North Macedonia,
Jordan, Tunisia, Guatemala, Peru, Sri Lanka, Indonesia, and
several others.

The Indonesian adaptation is the relevant deployment for the
toolkit. IREX partnered with Moonshot to produce Literata (the
online information-literacy course in Bahasa Indonesia) and
Gali Fakta, a 10-minute game embedded inside the curriculum.
Gali Fakta drops the player into a simulated family WhatsApp
group chat where members are sharing mixed accurate-and-false
content; the player double-checks each post and reports back,
losing credibility for wrong calls and building it for right
ones. The pedagogical design choices (WhatsApp-group framing,
peer-driven scenarios, helping-friends-and-family motivation)
are deliberately tuned to Indonesian digital habits.

For the fact-checker, IREX L2D is the broader methodology layer
that a media-literacy programme builds on. Where Tempo Detektif
Deepfake covers the synthetic-content surface specifically, IREX
L2D covers manipulative-information recognition more broadly,
and the two complement each other in a complete audience-
engagement deployment.

## When to use it

- A regional fact-check organisation is launching or expanding a
 formal media-literacy programme and needs a curriculum
 methodology with documented effectiveness rather than starting
 from scratch.
- A trainer in Indonesia is preparing a workshop for community
 volunteers, secondary-school students, or family-network
 outreach and wants both a curriculum (Literata) and an
 interactive game (Gali Fakta) for the same audience.
- An institutional partner needs to cite a methodology with
 peer-reviewed effectiveness research for funder reporting or
 grant-renewal documentation.
- A trainer outside Indonesia is adapting a similar curriculum
 for Thai, Filipino, Sinhala, Tamil, Malay, or Lao audiences
 and needs the underlying methodology architecture as a
 starting point.

## Limitations

!!! info "Limitations"
    - Limited SEA language coverage. Indonesian
    adaptation (Gali Fakta) exists; no Thai, Malay, Filipino,
    Sinhala, Tamil, or Lao adaptations are documented in the
    inventory. The toolkit's cell-level honest-gap (G-051)
    names this absence and the operational route is partner-
    led adaptation rather than direct deployment.
    - Effectiveness studies, while documented, require careful
    interpretation. The Facciani et al. (Humanities and
    Social Sciences Communications, February 2026) cross-
    cultural study found Gali Fakta significantly improved
    sharing discernment among Indonesian participants but
    did not significantly enhance accuracy discernment in the
    same population; the headline figures from earlier IREX
    reports describe the effects of the broader L2D
    programme rather than the game alone.
    - Curriculum-style deployment requires trainer time and
    community partner commitment beyond what a one-click
    game deployment requires; the methodology is most
    effective in sustained programme contexts rather than
    single-session exposure.
    - Inoculation effects from media-literacy interventions
    generally decay without reinforcement; IREX's
    programme structure includes booster components but
    sustained delivery requires sustained funding.

## Privacy and threat model

The Indonesian deployment runs through Literata.id and the Gali
Fakta game embedded there; Moonshot operates the technical
infrastructure on IREX's behalf. Players load the site in the
browser and play the game without account creation; standard
web analytics apply. The methodology itself is curriculum-based
rather than data-driven, so there is no source-protection
question intrinsic to deployment.

The deployment-side threat model is curriculum-and-context
rather than data-flow: a trainer running IREX L2D in a
politically sensitive context (Sri Lanka pre-election, an
Indonesian election period, a Lao diaspora community) should
think about how participation in the training is documented and
how the trainer's own visibility matters to the participants'
safety. IREX's institutional methodology covers some of this
ground; local adaptation often needs more.

## Country and platform applicability

- **Indonesia:** primary deployment via Literata.id and
 Gali Fakta, with measured effectiveness in the Facciani et
 al. 2026 study (sharing discernment improvement among
 Indonesian participants; Bahasa-tuned WhatsApp-group framing).
 Cited 25-percentage-point multi-source-checking improvement
 per RAND RCT references the broader IREX L2D programme rather
 than Gali Fakta alone.
- **Laos:** not adapted; the cell-level honest-gap on
 localised prebunking outside Indonesia applies. The
 methodology architecture could in principle be adapted by
 Lao diaspora or regional partners.
- **Malaysia:** not adapted in Malay; partner-led
 adaptation is the operational route. Sebenarnya.my or MAFINDO-
 Malaysia could plausibly lead.
- **Philippines:** not adapted in Filipino; the
 #FactsFirstPH coalition or a VERA Files-led partnership could
 plausibly lead.
- **Sri Lanka:** documented L2D online campaign
 deployment cited by IREX alongside Indonesia and Serbia
 (regional case documentation Lanka by reference; the L2D online
 campaign is a campaign-format adaptation, not a Literata-
 style course in Sinhala or Tamil). Hashtag Generation and
 Watchdog could plausibly lead a fuller curriculum adaptation.
- **Thailand:** not adapted in Thai; Cofact Thailand or
 AFP Fact Check Thailand could plausibly lead.

Platform applicability: web-based curriculum and game; sharable
through any platform a partner organisation uses to reach its
audience.

## How to access

Visit literata.id for the Indonesian curriculum and the embedded
Gali Fakta game. The IREX project page at irex.org/project/
learn-discern documents the broader methodology and the country
adaptations. The Trainer's Manual is available free from IREX;
institutional partnerships for new country adaptations are
arranged directly with IREX.

## Cost (current as of 2026-05)

Free for player and learner access at literata.id. The Trainer's
Manual is free. Country-adaptation costs (translation,
contextualisation, partner-organisation engagement, sustained
programme delivery) are absorbed by the deploying institution
and any IREX-managed funder; the toolkit cannot pin a figure for
a hypothetical Thai, Filipino, Sinhala, Tamil, Malay, or Lao
adaptation.

## Quickstart (deployment-side)

1. Identify the audience and the deployment frame: formal
 curriculum, community-volunteer training, online campaign,
 school programme.
2. For Indonesian audiences, route directly to Literata.id and
 the embedded Gali Fakta game; the existing deployment is the
 right starting point.
3. For non-Indonesian audiences, contact IREX's L2D programme
 to discuss adaptation; do not deploy the Bahasa Indonesian
 version into a non-Bahasa context as a substitute.
4. For curriculum integration, allow time for trainer
 preparation; the methodology is most effective when
 delivered by trainers familiar with the L2D approach rather
 than self-taught.
5. Build in reinforcement and booster sessions; single-exposure
 media-literacy effects decay without follow-up.
6. Pair the curriculum with complementary inoculation games
 from 2A.3 (Tempo Detektif Deepfake for Indonesia, Bad News
 architecture for adaptation work) and with awareness
 campaigns from Jigsaw Prebunking.

## In the toolkit's workflow

Source-history and behaviour pillar non-detector audience-
engagement deployment per Architectural Anchor 1. Sits in 2A.3
as the alternative-but-complementary methodology to Tempo Detektif Deepfake; Tempo's game covers synthetic-content
recognition specifically, IREX L2D covers manipulative-
information recognition more broadly, and a complete media-
literacy programme typically uses both.

Standard combinations (deployment combinations):

- With **Tempo Detektif Deepfake** at 2A.3 – IREX L2D
 curriculum frames the methodology, Tempo Detektif Deepfake
 provides the synthetic-content-specific interactive
 component.
- With **Jigsaw Prebunking** at 2A.3 – when the
 programme also wants short video-ad inoculation surfaces
 reaching passive audiences alongside the active curriculum.
- With **Bad News / Harmony Square** at 2A.3 – as the
 cross-cultural reference architecture (the Facciani et al.
 2026 study explicitly compares Gali Fakta and Harmony Square
 cross-culturally).
- With **Kalimasada** at 2B.1 – for an end-to-end
 Indonesia programme: Literata curriculum plus Gali Fakta
 game upstream, MAFINDO Kalimasada tipline downstream when
 trained audience members encounter content they want to
 verify.

Decision-tree references: this is not a verification routine;
the decision trees in T1–T7 do not invoke L2D at runtime. The
methodology sits in the editorial-strategic layer that shapes
audience capacity rather than per-claim handling.

This card carries no detector signal class: IREX L2D builds
audience media-literacy capacity; it does not produce a
synthetic-content verdict on any specific piece of suspect
content.
