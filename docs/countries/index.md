# Countries

The six country pages document each focus country's information environment, the documented cases that anchor the toolkit's regional examples, the language coverage paths, the legal and threat context, and the first-minute operational routing a working fact-checker reaches for when a case arrives with a country anchor. The intended reading order is whichever country is in your current queue – the pages are operational reference, not a regional survey. Cross-country observations sit inside the individual pages and not as a separate analytical layer; the cross-references at the bottom of each page surface the structural pairings that matter (Sri Lanka and Laos as the paired-gap countries under Decision 7, Indonesia and Malaysia as the Bahasa–Malay cross-border pair, Philippines and Thailand as the Pillar-1-detector versus Pillar-1-provenance contrast).

The pages are written in the voice of regional civil-society practitioners, anchored to documented cases and published research on the regional information environment. The framing is direct on what works and what does not: tooling coverage is asymmetric across SEA languages, tipline maturity is unevenly distributed, legal-risk environments shape verification workflow alongside the verification question itself.

## The six pages

[Indonesia](indonesia.md) – the densest civil-society fact-checking infrastructure in Southeast Asia, anchored on CekFakta and MAFINDO [Kalimasada](../tool-cards/mafindo-kalimasada.md), with the 2024 election cycle and the January–February 2025 Prabowo / Sri Mulyani deepfake cluster as the worked Pillar 2 reference case.

[Laos](laos.md) – the structural-gap country with diaspora-and-regional-partner routing as the operational baseline; no IFCN signatory, no Lao-first independent fact-checker, no Lao-language audio detector, with Decree 327 shaping the surveillance environment.

[Malaysia](malaysia.md) – Sinar Project monitoring infrastructure, the Sebenarnya AIFA government-operated chatbot (the toolkit's only B1 exception-pass), the Communications and Multimedia Act Section 233 oscillation through 2025–2026, and the cross-border Bahasa–Malay framing with Indonesia.

[Philippines](philippines.md) – the most-documented Pillar 1 detection casework in the region, anchored on Rappler / VERA Files / #FactsFirstPH and the Doc Willie Ong eye-drop deepfake; COMELEC Resolution 11064 as the clearest AI-election rule set in SEA; red-tagging and anti-terror prosecutions shaping the S2 surveillance surface.

[Sri Lanka](sri-lanka.md) – multilingual fact-check capacity with structural tipline thinness, anchored on the Sinhala/Tamil asymmetry; Watchdog, Hashtag Generation, FactCheck.lk, Fact Crescendo SL, and FactSeeker on the operational side; LIRNEasia and the University of Moratuwa on the language-tech side; the Online Safety Act 2024 and PTA legacy shaping the threat environment.

[Thailand](thailand.md) – LINE-native verification through [Cofact Thailand](../tool-cards/cofact-thailand.md); Thai PBS and SONP for public-broadcaster fact-checking; the February 2026 Anutin / Mauerberger [SynthID](../tool-cards/synthid-detector.md) case as the toolkit's clearest worked provenance-first verification; Article 112 lèse-majesté as the sharpest S2 routing surface in the toolkit; Pegasus surveillance history sitting central in the background risk picture.

## How the pages connect

The pages cross-reference each other on three structural patterns. The first is the paired-gap pattern between Sri Lanka and Laos under Decision 7 honest-gap policy – Sri Lanka has fact-check capacity but structural tipline thinness, Laos has neither at scale, and the toolkit names both directly instead of papering them over. The second is the Bahasa–Malay cross-border pattern between Indonesia and Malaysia – the two languages share substantial vocabulary and structure, the CekFakta–MAFINDO ecosystem and the Malaysian fact-check ecosystem have documented operational overlap, and cross-border claims often warrant coordinated verification. The third is the Pillar-1-detector versus Pillar-1-provenance contrast between the Philippines and Thailand – Doc Willie Ong runs the full Pillar 1 detector ladder, Anutin / Mauerberger runs provenance-first with SynthID and never invokes a detector, and the two cases together demonstrate Architectural Anchor 1 in operation.

Working fact-checkers, OSINT investigators, and civil-society staff in the six countries should read whichever country page is currently in their queue, follow the cross-references when a case crosses a regional border or shares structural shape with a neighbour, and use the operational routing sections as the first-minute handles when a country-specific case arrives.
